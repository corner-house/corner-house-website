## Who Is Sobha Limited?

Sobha Limited was founded in 1995 by P.N.C. Menon in Bengaluru. It is listed on both the Bombay Stock Exchange and the National Stock Exchange, making it one of the few Indian residential developers with publicly audited financials and corporate governance obligations.

The company's headquarters is in Bengaluru, where the largest portion of its delivered portfolio is concentrated. Over 30 years, Sobha has expanded to 27 cities across 14 Indian states. As of the company's most recent public disclosures, Sobha has completed 581 projects covering 150.13 million sqft.

The sobha developer track record is unusually verifiable by Indian real estate standards. Because Sobha is listed, its project launches, completions, revenue recognition, and financial health are part of a regulated public disclosure process. A buyer evaluating is sobha a good developer has access to information that simply does not exist for most private Indian developers.

## The No-Subcontracting Policy ,  What It Actually Means

The single most important structural differentiator in Sobha's construction model is backward integration. Where most Indian residential developers outsource construction to subcontractors, Sobha manufactures a significant portion of the construction components in-house.

Sobha's manufacturing operations include:

**Interiors:** Sobha manufactures its own interior components including joinery, furniture elements, and decorative items through its in-house manufacturing facilities.

**Glazing and metal works:** Facade glazing, aluminium works, and metal components are produced by Sobha's own manufacturing operations.

**Concrete products:** Sobha manufactures its own concrete products rather than procuring from external suppliers.

**Restoplus mattresses:** Sobha manufactures Restoplus mattresses in-house for its residential projects.

The implication for buyers is direct: when Sobha says a project will have a specific finish standard, the company controls the production of that finish. There is no subcontractor chain where quality can degrade between the developer's specification and the site execution.

The company states 1,456 quality checks before every handover. This figure is verifiable against Sobha's listed company disclosures and reflects the systematic quality verification that backward integration enables.

No Indian residential developer of equivalent scale has replicated this manufacturing depth. It is Sobha's most defensible competitive advantage and the foundation of the sobha quality construction claim.

## Financial Health ,  Why Listed Status Matters

Is sobha a good developer from a financial stability standpoint? Sobha Limited's listed company status provides an answer that private developers cannot.

As a BSE and NSE listed company, Sobha Limited publishes quarterly financial results, annual reports, and is subject to SEBI's disclosure requirements. Buyers can review Sobha's revenue recognition, debt levels, project completion rates, and management commentary in publicly available regulatory filings.

This financial transparency is not available for most Indian real estate developers, who are privately held and disclose only what they choose to. A buyer comparing Sobha Aranya against a project from a private developer is comparing a company with full public financial accountability against a company whose financial health is largely opaque.

The practical implication: if Sobha's financial position deteriorates, the listed-company disclosure requirements mean buyers and regulators can see it early. The combination of HARERA protection and listed-company financial visibility creates a buyer protection framework that is among the strongest available in Indian residential real estate.
