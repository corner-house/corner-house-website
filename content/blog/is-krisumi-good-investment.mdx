## Signal 1 ,  Sector 36A Corridor Fundamentals

Any honest answer to is krisumi a good investment or is sector 36a gurgaon investment worth making must start with the corridor, not the project. The project cannot be stronger than the infrastructure it sits in.

Sector 36A on the Dwarka Expressway has three structural advantages that support the krisumi city investment case.

**The expressway is fully operational.** The Northern Peripheral Road became fully operational in 2019, removing the single biggest risk that investors in this corridor had carried for a decade. You can drive from Sector 36A to NH-48 in under 10 minutes on a normal day. The infrastructure risk that plagued early Dwarka Expressway investments is gone.

**Airport connectivity is real.** IGI Airport is 24.8 km from Krisumi City, mostly via the expressway with minimal signals. The drive runs 30 to 40 minutes depending on time of day. For the NRI buyer and the frequent-travel executive, this is a genuine advantage over Golf Course Road which sits farther from the airport.

**Employment catchment is broad.** Cyber City in DLF Phase 2 is 17 km, approximately 25 minutes. The Manesar industrial corridor is accessible via NH-48. The buyer who works in Gurgaon's tech and financial services belt and travels internationally regularly finds Sector 36A geography sensible.

The corridor weakness is equally real and must be named. Sector 36A does not yet have the density of social infrastructure that Golf Course Road or Golf Course Extension Road offer. Schools, hospitals, and daily retail are still maturing. The nearest established retail is Sapphire 83 Mall, approximately 3 km away. For buyers who need a fully serviced neighbourhood around them from day one of possession in 2030, Sector 36A will be better than today but may not match the Golf Course corridors.

## Signal 3 ,  HARERA Registration and Legal Protection

The third signal is legal. Is krisumi a good investment from a buyer protection standpoint?

Krisumi Forest Reserve carries two active HARERA registrations: RC/REP/HARERA/GGM/944/676 for Phase I and RC/REP/HARERA/GGM/945/677 for Phase II. Both are verifiable on haryanarera.gov.in. This matters because HARERA registration provides specific legal protections that unregistered projects cannot offer.

Under HARERA, if Krisumi Corporation fails to deliver possession by November 2030, buyers are entitled to interest at the State Bank of India MCLR rate plus 2% per annum on every rupee paid, calculated from the committed date until actual possession. The developer cannot unilaterally delay without financial consequence. This changes the risk calculus fundamentally compared to pre-RERA projects where delays had no legal remedy.

The krisumi city investment case is stronger because of HARERA, not despite it. The project is registered, the possession date is legally committed, and the quarterly compliance mechanism means the government can see if the developer is falling behind schedule before it becomes a crisis.

For buyers comparing is krisumi a good investment against projects that are not HARERA registered or where HARERA registration is claimed but not verifiable on the portal, the comparison should start and end with the HARERA status. An unregistered luxury project is not a comparable investment regardless of the renders or the marketing.

## The Risks ,  Named Directly

An honest krisumi city investment analysis cannot stop at the positives. Here are the specific risks.

**Four-year construction wait.** Possession is November 2030. If you book today, your capital is committed to an under-construction asset for approximately 4 years with no rental income during that period. This is not a risk unique to Krisumi ,  it is the standard condition of new-launch luxury real estate. But it is a real cost that must be factored into the return calculation.

**Resale liquidity at Rs 7 Crore plus.** The all-in budget for a Krisumi Forest Reserve 3 LDK exceeds Rs 7 Crore. The resale buyer pool for a property at that price in Sector 36A is smaller than it would be at Rs 3 to 4 Crore. If you need to exit before possession for any reason, finding a buyer at the right price in a corridor that is still maturing will take time. This is a krisumi forest reserve worth buying question that investors with a shorter horizon need to answer honestly.

**GST on the full construction period.** GST at 5% applies on every payment made to Krisumi Corporation because the project is under construction. On a Rs 6 to 7 Crore base price, that is Rs 30 to 40 Lakh in GST that a ready-to-move unit does not carry. This cost is real and must be included in your return calculation.

**Sector 36A social infrastructure gap.** Named above. If the corridor does not mature as expected by 2030, end-use buyers at possession may find the neighbourhood less convenient than anticipated. Investors who plan to sell or rent out the unit depend on the neighbourhood being attractive to the next buyer or tenant.

## Our Verdict

Is krisumi a good investment? Here is the Corner House verdict, with the buyer profile specified.

**Yes, for buyers with a 4 to 7 year horizon** who are comfortable committing Rs 7 Crore plus to an under-construction asset, who want ultra-luxury quality in an infrastructure-strong Gurgaon corridor, and who have the financial headroom to hold through the 4-year construction period without needing rental income or liquidity from this asset. For this buyer, the three signals ,  corridor fundamentals, developer track record, and HARERA protection ,  all support the investment.

**Conditional yes for end-users** who plan to move into the property at possession in November 2030. The answer depends on how much the Sector 36A social infrastructure matures between now and 2030. If you visited the neighbourhood today and found it too sparse, factor in 4 years of corridor development before making that judgment. If the expressway and airport proximity are more important to you than walkable schools and retail, Sector 36A is a strong end-use choice by 2030.

**No for buyers needing liquidity within 3 years**, buyers whose total investable assets are more than 50% committed to this single property, or buyers who need rental income to service the cost of the asset during construction. The 4-year wait with no income is a hard constraint.

Is krisumi a good investment? For the right buyer, with eyes open on the risks named above, the answer is yes.
